# Alumni Nexus  
Alumni Directory & Communication Portal

## Overview
Alumni Nexus is a secure, web-based Alumni Directory and Communication Portal designed to centralise alumni information and strengthen alumni–student engagement at Durban University of Technology (DUT).

Currently, alumni data at DUT is fragmented across departments and faculties, limiting structured networking, mentorship, and career development opportunities. With six faculties operating independently, there is no unified system that connects alumni to their respective faculty and department.

Alumni Nexus addresses this challenge by providing a single, centralised platform that supports structured networking, mentorship, communication, and measurable engagement across the institution.

---

## Purpose
The purpose of Alumni Nexus is to:
- Centralise alumni data across all faculties and departments
- Enable faculty- and department-based routing of alumni interactions
- Promote structured networking and professional mentorship
- Enhance career guidance for current students
- Strengthen long-term relationships between alumni and the institution

---

## Solution Overview
Alumni Nexus incorporates a structured networking framework that prioritises usability, clarity, and data integrity. The platform is built around three core components:

### 1. Connection System
The Connection System enables alumni and students to establish professional relationships through a controlled request-and-approval process.

Key features include:
- Search users by faculty, department, industry, graduation year, and skills
- Send, accept, or decline connection requests
- Enable internal communication only after mutual approval
- Maintain privacy, security, and role-based access control

This ensures meaningful and secure professional networking.

---

### 2. Mentorship Module
The Mentorship Module formalises the mentoring process within the institution.

Features include:
- Alumni registration as mentors with defined expertise and availability
- Students browsing mentors within their faculty or department
- Structured mentorship request workflow with status tracking
- Accountability and organised academic and career support

This transforms informal mentorship into a structured, trackable development process.

---

### 3. DUT Pinboard
The DUT Pinboard is designed to support event-based alumni engagement and generate measurable insights.

Features include:
- Alumni and administrators posting faculty- or department-specific events
- Events such as webinars, workshops, and networking sessions
- Student RSVP functionality
- Attendance recording by event hosts
- Engagement metrics comparing RSVPs and actual attendance
- Dashboard insights showing participation trends per faculty or department

This ensures alumni engagement initiatives are both facilitated and evaluated.
